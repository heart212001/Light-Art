smoke photography

Choose the smoke source. One of my favorite sources of smoke for this type of photography is incense, because one stick
will burn for 45 minutes, giving you plenty of time to take some perfect pictures

Place your smoke source on a table. Choose a wall for your background and place a small table in front of it.
Place the incense on the table, about a meter (about three feet) in front of the wall

Single black canvas on the wall.

Lamp preparation. In order for smoke to be photographed, it must be properly lit.
Take a halogen flashlight or incandescent lamp and place it in front of the table, to the right or left of the incense burner.
Think of the incense as the center of a clock and on the wall at 12:00: Put the lamp at 3 or 9 o'clock.

Put the reflector on the other side like a cork.

Control the items in the room. In order to prevent smoke from dispersing, close all windows, turn off fans, and close anything that might create a draft in the room.
Similarly, to make the smoke more visible and easier to photograph, close the doors, the blinds, and turn off all the extra lights in the room.

Fire your smoke source. Light an incense, cigarette or candle. To light the incense, hold the flame on the ledge so that you catch fires.
Let the burner burn for a moment until it glows red, then blow out the flame.
